v17 / Chinese Courtyard Garden

A moon garden retold as a Chinese courtyard

Shanglin in the lantern court at night.

This version keeps the storybook garden spirit from v07, but places it inside a Chinese courtyard world of pavilions, moon gates, tiled roofs, shadowed corridors, and warm lantern light. The mood is quieter, deeper, and more architectural.

moon gate lantern court ink garden

Front Court

The player moving through the first courtyard.

Games still shape how I enter a space: reading tension, waiting for the right opening, and knowing when one careful move becomes commitment. The garden is gentle, but the mind inside it still pays attention like a player.

Here that instinct feels less like battle and more like crossing a stone path beneath lanterns, where every turn reveals a slightly different arrangement of pressure and calm.

Side Pavilion

The scholar tracing hidden preference like paths through a court.

Recommender systems belong naturally in this landscape. They are another way of moving through layered space, finding relation, surprise, and order in patterns that first appear scattered.

Glass Hall

The singer where the night air stays warm.

Singing keeps the courtyard alive. It turns structure into breath, keeps emotion present inside discipline, and lets the whole page feel inhabited rather than only arranged.
